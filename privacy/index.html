Privacy

The short version: PlusPlus has no accounts and no servers. Your training data is yours, on your device — and, only if you choose, in a GitHub repository you own.

WHAT PLUSPLUS COLLECTS

Nothing. PlusPlus has no sign-up, no user accounts, and operates no backend servers. The app does not send your data to us — there is no "us" to send it to. We do not use analytics SDKs, advertising identifiers, or tracking of any kind.

WHERE YOUR DATA LIVES

On your device

Routines, exercises, and workout history are stored locally on your iPhone (and mirrored to your Apple Watch for workouts in progress). Deleting the app deletes this data.

In a repo you own (optional)

If you enable GitHub sync or use export, your data is written as JSON to a repository in your GitHub account, under GitHub's terms and your control. PlusPlus never has server-side access to it.

In links you share (optional)

Sharing a routine encodes it directly into the link itself. The routine travels inside the URL fragment, which is never sent to any server — including ours. Only the people you send the link to can see it.

APPLE FRAMEWORKS

Notifications are local (rest timers, schedule reminders) — no push servers. If a future version integrates Apple Health, health data will be read and written only with your explicit permission, stay on-device, and never be transmitted anywhere; this policy will be updated before that ships.

THIS SITE

plusplus.fit is a static site. It sets no cookies and runs no analytics. Shared-routine pages decode their content entirely in your browser.

CONTACT

Questions? Open an issue on GitHub — the app is open source, so you can also just read what it does.
